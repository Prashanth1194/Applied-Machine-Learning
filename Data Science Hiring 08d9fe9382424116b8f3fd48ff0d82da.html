    • Assignment criteria

      For the assignment, a minimum is looking for OK's across all categories and few weaks. If one section is particularly strong this still counts throughout; especially if it looks like quality is decreasing towards the end (likely if a candidate is time pressured)

      There is some levelling expected here: a more junior applicant (aligned with CV and call) would be expected to hit OKs, but a more experienced candidate must have some strongs and less weaks

      Typically:

      Level 2: All OK's, some strongs, few weaks

      Level 3: All OK's, some strongs, one / two really strongs , few weaks

      • Definition of retention

        Pros:

        1. Ok: Thought about business context to problem
        1. Strong: Thought about time period and how to define (daily, 7 daily, 30 day) and impact of decision
        1. Strong: Discussed app versus transaction retention
        1. Really Strong: Evidence of data led decision for time period
        1. Really Strong: Calculated app versus transaction retention
        1. Strong: Recommendations about what this means for levels of user engagement

        Cons:

        1. Weak: No context and arbitrary time period
        1. Weak: Graphs and no explanation
        1. Weak: Blends transaction and app data into a soup metric
      • Unskewed analysis

        Pros:

        1. Ok: Links transaction definition from question (1) to problem
        1. Ok: Cohort analysis
        1. Strong: Recommendations from cohort analysis about how we can develop product
        1. Strong: Thinks about what it means to compare cohorts

        Cons:

        1. Weak: Defaults to a machine learning / predictive problem
        1. Really Weak: Doesn't think about timeliness of answer, defaults to 28 days to respond
      • Segment comparison

        Pros:

        1. Ok: Profiling of 2 + factors
        1. Strong: Thinks about correlation versus causation
        1. Strong: Multiple profiling of different types of factors
        1. Strong: Recommendations on how we can use profiling
        1. Really Strong: Multivariate model to analyse.
        1. Really, Really Strong: Survival model

        Cons:

        1. Weak: Defaulted to just profiling age
        1. Weak: Defaulted to a predictive ML problem

      • Code quality

        Pros:

        1. Ok: Well formatted SQL
        1. Strong: CTE's used in SQL expressions
        1. Really Strong: Connected Python and used push down SQL evaluation

        Cons:

        1. Weak: Lots of random SQL files
        1. Weak: Lots of horrible nested subqueries
      • Format

        Pros:

        1. Ok: Presentation as well as code
        1. Strong: Recommendations clearly visible
        1. Strong: Good clear visuals, clear slide layout

        Cons:

        1. Weak: Notebooks only, not digestible
        1. Weak: Irrelevant graphs
        1. Weak: Endless feature profiling

  • 4Ô∏è‚É£¬†Task Review
    • When do I do one?

      A task review call happens when we have either a really strong first stage and a weak assignment, or an assignment that showed promise but wasn't strong enough to pass through on its own.

    • Structure

      The main structure of these tends to be:

      1. Make sure to thank the candidate for their time in doing the assignment - people have to juggle these with their work or private lives
      1. Identify the weak areas of the assessment (refer to the previous assessment scorecard!, but normally don't jump straight into specific sections where possible, try to pull out themes!. For example, if the assignment is light on recommendations, ask them 'What are the top 3 things Monzo should be doing differently as a result of this work!'. Common patterns tend to be:
        1. Probe on recommendations
        1. Probe on causality ('how do I know x causes y')
        1. Concise explanations ('tell me the top findings in your work')
        1. How do we react faster or proxy the behaviour more
      1. Make sure to ask one question at a time! You'll normally only have 30 minutes to probe, so if a user has suggested a complicated approach (like incentivising behaviour) its best to probe on the areas that we'd care most about. In the incentives case, instead of asking 'how do we know if it works?' ask 'Incentives sound expensive! How do I know if it adds more value than it costs?'

      üé≤¬†Data Science - Initial Call Rubric for L40 and L50 IC

      Basic outline of the interview

      There are two key parts to an initial call: getting a feel for the candidate‚Äôs skills and selling the candidate on Monzo and the role.

      This will be the first time many candidates have spoken to someone in Data at Monzo - its incredibly important that they land with a good first impression.

      For the most part the initial call is for testing that the essentials on the CV are noted and demonstrable face to face. In some cases a candidate may be levelled out if their CV is low on experience and they struggle to hit requirements for next role

      A suggested interview structure is:

      1. 0 - 5 minutes: Greet the candidate and do introductions. Tell them about who you are, your role at Monzo. Get them to introduce themselves
      1. 5 - 7 minutes: explain the structure of the interview, that there will be two parts, one letting the candidate know about the job role in more detail, then going into their background. Let them know that you‚Äôll save at least 5 minutes at the end for all questions
      1. 7 - 15 minutes: Go over basic context on the role(s). Give the candidate an opportunity to get excited. If they came through a generic job ab, its a good chance to sell them on the space that‚Äôs the best fit.
      1. 15 - 40 minutes: Ask questions (see below)
      1. 40 - 45 minutes: Give candidate opportunity to ask their remaining questions. You can see below for the typical questions asked - its worth preparing yourself for these.
